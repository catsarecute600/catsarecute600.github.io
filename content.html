Endangered Animals used in Quiz:

Panda

  1. Pandas mate in spring.
  2. The female will be pregnant for 100 to 180 days before giving birth.One or two cubs will be born. The cubs will only weight 3 to 5 ounces.
  3. Pandas are usually loners because they don't like being around other pandas. Their good smell helps them to know when there is another panda near so they can avoid them.
  4. They eat bamboo for 12 hours a day.
  5. They distinguished by their large size and their black-white color.
  6. They are usually 4 to 5 feet tall and weigh up to 300 pounds.

Sea Otter

  1. They eat about 25% of their weight in food each day to support their high metabolism.
  2. There used to be several hundred thousands to millions of sea otters, but now only about 106,000 remain.
  3. A lot of times sea otters are in water and when they are trying to catch food, they can dive up to 330 feet in the water
  4. They can mate at anytime of the year. They are usually pregnant for 6 to 8 months before giving birth to pups. They usually give birth to one pup, but they can also give birth to twins.
  5. Sea otters are the second smallest marine mammals.
  6. Their role in their environment has a greater effect than other species.

    7. Snow Leopard

    1. It is estimated that only 3,500 to 7000 snow leopards remain in the wild. There are also 600 to 700 snow leopards in zoos.
    2. They are found at altitudes between 9,800 and 17,000 feet.
    3. They prefer to inhabit steep cliff areas, rocky outcrops and ravines.
    4. They stalk for their prey from a distance of 20 to 50 feet in spring.
    5. They mate in January to mid-March
    6. They are pregnant for 3 to 3 1/2 months before giving birth. They usually give birth to 2 to 3 cubs.

    Malayan Tiger

    1. They are usually 6.5ft - 7.8ft long and weight 176 - 330 pounds.
    2. They can live 18 to 25 years.
    3. They are usually solitary.
    4. The female is pregnant for 3 to 4 months before giving birth.
    5. The average litter size is 3 cubs, but the female can give birth to up to 5 cubs.
    6. Their top speed is 60mph.

    Beluga Whale

    1. Belugas are the smallest whale. They sometimes look like big dolphins. They weigh around 2000 to 3000 pounds and can become 13 to 20 feet long.
    2. Their skin is covered with fur.
    3. They have thick blubber and thick skin to help them adapt to freezing water.
    4. They can stay underwater for 25 minutes.
    5. They are social animals and live in groups called pods.
    6. They are brown or black at birth, but when they are 5 to 8 years old, their color begins to fade.

    Monarch Butterfly

    1. Where milkweed is, there will be Monarch butterflies.
    2. They can produce 4 generations in one summer. The first three generation will live 2 to 6 weeks, but the fourth generation can live up to 9 months.
    3. Millions of monarch butterflies make the trip down to Mexico to roost for the winter.
    4. Their wind span is 3 3/8 to 4 7/8 inches.
    5. Monarchs are toxic to birds because they eat milkweed.
    6. They flap their wings 5 to 12 times a second.

Mystic Creatures used in Quiz:

Dragons

  1. Dragons are often fire-breathing, reptilelike creatures with wings, huge claws, and a long tail.
  2. China and Japan view dragons as good or in a positive light.
  3. They appear in many Greek and Roman myths.
  4. In Chinese and Japanese mythology and tradition, dragons symbolize power, happiness, and fertility and are believed to bring good fortune and wealth.

Unicorns

  1. The unicorn is said to hold the power to divine truth and will pierce the heart of a liar with its horn.
  2. A unicorn has immense power and strength.
  3. A horse with a single straight horn projecting from its forehead; it has a twisted horn, a deer's feet, a goat's beard, and a lion's tail.
  4. It's horn possesses medicinal or magical properties.

    5. Phoenixes

    1. A phoenix is said to be a mythical bird with red,yellow,orange feathers and a gold and scarlet tail.
    2. It is a large bird, blessed with supernatural power.
    3. They are believed to live for 500 years.
    4. In the Chinese it is called the Great Empress.

    Griffins

    1. A griffin is portrayed as a mix between an eagle and a lion.
    2. A griffin in mythology is a creature with the body and regal, kingly mythical creature who commanded deep respect.
    3. They were thought to be very wise and wily characters who spent a good deal of time seeking out and guarding gold and treasures.
    4. Other legends protray griffins as a trickster.

    Sphinxes

    1. A sphinx is a mythical creature with the body of a lion, most often with a human head and sometimes with wings.
    2. Sphinxes were first created by the Egyptians and usually wore a head-dresses.
    3. Sphinxes were commonly represented in both Assyrian and Persian art, with wings and human heads.
    4. The ancient Greeks saw the sphinx as a more troublesome creature.

    Fairy

    1. Fairies are supernatural beings endowed with the powers of magic and enchantment.
    2. Fairies like all things sparkly.
    3. Fairies bring good luck wherever they go.
    4. Fairies can speak many languages.
